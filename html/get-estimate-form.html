What is a modular kitchen cost calculator?

A straightforward yet effective tool, the modular kitchen cost calculator gives you an overview and an approximation of the implementation costs related to custom-curated kitchens. It is simple to use, effective, and transparent, giving you important information about changes in cost related to floor plans as well as materials and design

It is crucial to remember that the real cost of implementation could differ, and for more assistance or information, one should always speak with the professionals at Design9 Space World.

How does the modular kitchen cost calculator assist you in determining how much the interior design of your kitchen will cost?

Based on your own preferences for the layout of your house, the style of the kitchen, the kind of cabinetry, the finish and material of the shutters, and the kitchen accessories, the Modular Kitchen Cost Calculator estimates the approximate cost of installing a modular kitchen. Once your specifications are selected, the calculator adds up all of the charges and compares them to give you an estimated final cost.

The procedures below assist you in selecting your requirements and provide you an approximate budget for the same:
  1. Choose a City.
  2. Choose your home configuration
  3. Select the desired shape for your kitchen.
  4. Select your preferred cabinet material.
  5. Select the Finish and Material of your choice for shutters.
  6. To round off the look of your kitchen, add accessories.

How should I select the material for my kitchen cabinets?

Cabinets are a necessary component of the kitchen and significantly improve its appearance in addition to serving as a major organiser and storage solution. Consequently, it's critical to spend money on high-quality, aesthetically pleasing, and durable cabinet materials.

Wood fibres are bonded together to create particle board. With the exception of washbasin units, they are moisture-resistant and suitable for use with all kitchen equipment. The two most common materials for kitchen cabinets are plywood and particle board.

How do I select the material for kitchen shutters?

The market offers a vast array of materials for kitchen shutters. For a more thorough explanation on the subject, click this link.

  1. HDHMR (High Density Heat Moisture Resistant) : This is a wood product made by compressing wooden fibers. It is durable, resistant to moisture damage and economical. Good for base cabinets.
  2. M Plywood : Made from multiple thin layers of wood bonded together. Very strong and rigid. Used for cabinet boxes and shelves.
  3. MDF (Medium Density Fiberboard) : Made from wood fibers bonded with resins. Smooth surface good for laminates. Used for door fronts, panels. Less expensive than plywood.
  4. Laminates : Paper or vinyl layer fused onto particle board or MDF. Durable and affordable. Provides wide range of finishes like wood, stone.
  5. Acrylic : Plastic sheets that can be colored or printed on. Glossy finish. Scratch resistant. Higher cost than laminates.
  6. Rehau : 2mm thick acrylic sheets. Rehau is a brand known for quality acrylic. Provides upscale finish.
  7. Action TESA UV : UV cured urethane coating applied on acrylic shutters. Glossy, scratch-resistant.
  8. Action TESA Acrylic : Water-based acrylic coating on acrylic shutters. Smooth finish. Eco-friendly.
  9. PU :Polyurethane coating on glass shutters. Glossy finish. Water and stain resistant.
What distinguishes the material of kitchen shutters from that of kitchen cabinets?

A cabinet is the carcass, or body, of your modular kitchen. Located on the front of the cabinet, the shutter is made of different materials than the carcass. The shutters in your kitchen are the most visible element, therefore you should spend money on them. Find out more about kitchen cabinets and shutters.
